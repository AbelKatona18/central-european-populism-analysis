Honourable Presidents! Your wives! Mr Speaker! Distinguished guests from across the border, ladies and gentlemen!
The weatherman announces at the end of the news: ‘Before I give you tomorrow’s forecast, I’d like to amend today’s forecast and apologise for yesterday’s.’ It’s a related profession, a tough job, but there is a difference. If the weather forecaster gets it wrong, at worst we’ll get soaked. If the Prime Minister gets it wrong, we won’t need an umbrella, but a lifeboat. It’s hard to say what tomorrow will bring, and in politics, even what yesterday brought. I learnt from Imre Pozsgay that nothing is as changeable as the past. He should know… That’s why every year, when I sit down to write my annual review, I read last year’s first. I still belong to the old school; I like it when what I say today is in line with what I said yesterday. That’s no longer the fashion. I remember the shock it caused thirty years ago when Gyula Horn was confronted with a previous statement of his, and he replied: ‘So what?’ When our opponents today are confronted with the fact that they are lying, they simply reply: that was yesterday, today is today. I suppose that’s what they call progress. We shouldn’t be surprised if many people don’t want to be up to date.
To cut a long story short, I’m having an easy day today, because last year I said the following: “Great opportunities are opening up before us. By the end of the year, the stage of world politics will present a completely different picture from what it does now, at the start of the year, and if God helps us, Hungary’s room for manoeuvre will not diminish, but will expand to an extent not seen in a long time.” And that is exactly what happened. From the President’s resignation in February, we moved on to victory in the European Parliament elections in June. War here, inflation there, yet across Europe Fidesz–KDNP won with the second-highest share of the vote. Only Malta saw a bigger victory, but what happens in Malta stays in Malta. The word ‘patriot’ is heard louder in Brussels today than ever before. This is a big deal, because speaking of patriotism in Brussels requires the same death-defying courage as standing in the Fradi B stand wearing a purple scarf.
Ladies and Gentlemen!
Last year, we stood here with heavy hearts. We had lost our President of the Republic and our lead candidate for the European Parliament, and the war was looking increasingly bleak. The economic outlook was also grim due to sanctions, inflation and energy prices. It was like a nightmare. The US ambassador took the lead of the opposition, we were under fire from Washington and Brussels, whilst George Soros’s agents here at home were busily setting fire to haystacks and poisoning the wells. And, as is often the case, betrayal was not far behind. It turned out that from here, from the front row of this annual review, the road to Mr Weber’s coffers in Brussels is not so long after all. This is a good lesson. He who betrays his friends betrays his party too. A person of this sort will betray anyone at the first opportunity. Would they not betray their own country? The virtue and strength of our community lie precisely in its loyalty. As our name suggests. Fidesz: faith, loyalty, trust. For those who do not understand this, or fail to appreciate its beauty, there is plenty of room elsewhere. In the end, everyone will get what they deserve. That is the law.
My dear friends!
No matter how the skies rumbled before the European elections, we remained calm, because we have learnt that time matters. Time is experience. We know that in politics there is only one constant: change. Something unpredictable always happens. Sometimes good, sometimes bad. And just when you think you’ve seen it all, that’s when the surprise usually comes. Bang! We dish out blows, and we take blows. Sometimes we take more than we give. The secret is to stay on your feet even then. We know from Laci Papp that boxing is a sport where even the winner gets beaten up badly. And that is exactly how it is in politics too. There is no victory without suffering . Pain is our friend. Then victory makes up for everything. You know too that this is the best medicine. And we only have to wait 14 months for the next one.
Ladies and gentlemen!
2024 was a manly year. We have seen that if we pull together, Hungary can do it. Yesterday we were heretics; today half the world is knocking on our door. They said we were the past. It turns out we are the future. We are evergreen, like the Rolling Stones. What is good never goes out of fashion.
My dear friends!
We have been fighting for fifteen years. A handful of Hungarian rebels against an empire. Alone, isolated, against the wind. The frightened Székely lad says to his father: “They’re coming, and there are ten times as many of them as there are of us!” To which the old man replies: “Well, that’s how many it takes!” Looking back from the cooling shade of the American election victory, of course, memory embellishes everything. But we know the situation was heated. As we say in the changing room: the pie was hot. The stakes were high. It’s quite a task to play red ulti with three cards for years on end; it takes nerves of steel.
My friends!
Let us be proud that we Hungarians played a significant part in changing the world. Far beyond our size, economic strength and population. We were the pioneers, the harbingers and the initiators of this uprising. Father Pio predicted that Hungary was a cage from which a beautiful bird would one day fly free. Much suffering still awaits them, he wrote, but they will share in glory unmatched throughout Europe. Perhaps that is what he meant? At times it was incredibly difficult, and there were moments when it seemed utterly hopeless. I am not talking about Fidesz, nor about the government, but about the Hungarian people. The Hungarian nation stood behind us throughout, both collectively and individually. We must bow our heads in respect for the Hungarians’ perseverance and determination. They never gave up for a single moment, they never backed down, and not once did they say we should give in to Soros. Not once did they say we should surrender to Brussels. My thanks to every rebellious Hungarian who, through their diligence, hard work and tenacity, defended the homeland against the empire! I am grateful to be able to serve such a people. Every politician in the world can envy this. Come what may, we have given the country a new, Christian, national constitution; we have protected ourselves from migration; we have protected our children from gender activists; we have stood up for peace and kept ourselves away from war. We have protected Hungary from the Soros crowd, foreign currency borrowers from the banks, and families from skyrocketing utility bills. We have created job opportunities for an extra million people, meaning that 4.7 million people are now in work. Never before have so many people been in work in Hungary. Caution, of course, is never amiss. When the Pope was asked how many people work in the Vatican, he replied: “about half.” Since I have mentioned the Holy Father, we thank him for being with us under the banner of peace. We wish him a speedy recovery from here too!
Dear Friends!
This year will be different. We are now walking down the main street of history, whilst our opponents are wandering about in muddy backstreets on the outskirts. I looked at our European allies in the photos from the Paris meeting. It was as if they’d been bitten by a wasp. The European Union is outraged that negotiations began without it and wants to sit down at the table. Sándor Demján told me: “If you want to sit down at the table where the big players are, look around and find the sucker. If you can’t find one, then know this: you are the one.” So much for Paris.
Ladies and gentlemen!
After last year’s gloomy ballad, this year brings hard-hitting rock and roll. You’d better fasten your seatbelts. The struggle continues, but there is one important difference. Now the goal is not to outmanoeuvre, not to evade, not to survive, but to win. Until now we have been rebelling; now we want to win as well. Following Hungary, the United States has also risen up. This immediately changes the situation. But let us not believe that America’s successful rebellion will bring victory for Hungary. They cannot win for us; they can only improve our chances. President Trump is not our saviour, but our comrade-in-arms. What’s more, even he hasn’t finished his work; he’s only just got started. Serious struggles still lie ahead for him – not just in world politics, but at home as well. From Hungary’s perspective, what has happened is that, whilst David was fighting Goliath, David’s brother has also arrived, and he looks like a rather burly lad. We have been given a chance to break out of the besieged castle. Indeed, not just to break out, but to break through the empire’s defences. Now is the time to think boldly and on a grand scale. That is why I propose to you, and to ourselves, that 2025 should be the year of the breakthrough.
My dear friends!
Let us not become complacent with last year’s successes. Although our opponents have suffered a serious blow, and for the first time I see fear in their eyes, and for the first time they are forced to retreat, it would be a mistake to underestimate them. Even so, even under these circumstances, we can only break through with a disciplined and well-planned military manoeuvre. Let us see what we must do. The empire has two heads and a central warehouse. One head is in Washington, one in Brussels, and the Soros-style warehouse is here in Budapest. We know this because the Americans have thrown the skeletons out of the cupboard. They have exposed and brought to light that thoroughly corrupt, oppressive power machine which siphoned billions from the US budget to pseudo-civil society organisations, bought-off journalists, judges and prosecutors, politicians, foundations, bureaucrats – a vast machinery that operated a liberal dictatorship of opinion and political oppression throughout the Western world, including Hungary. This is the truth. It turned out that nothing was what they said it was. Tolerance, diversity, sensitivity training, civil society organisations, equal opportunities, the rule of law… Come on! It was exactly what we Hungarians have always seen it to be: a robust financial and power machine, created to crush, grind down and devour the freedom and independence of nations, so that the empire might survive. The empire on top, the nations below, until they squeeze the life out of us and the empire’s eternal order is established. And it has been like this since time immemorial. “We’ve been down here for so long, we don’t even know what it’s like up there,” says Hobo, and he’s right. Hungary has been in opposition to Brussels for fifteen years. We have to be in opposition to the government. Even Chuck Norris could give that a go. It would be to his credit, even though he has a four-fifths majority.
My dear friends!
It’s a fine and inspiring thing that’s happening in America, but let that be the Americans’ concern. We Hungarians must now row home from international waters and attend to our own affairs. First of all, we must deal with the empire’s Budapest depot. The method is called: full steam ahead. A high ball comes in, feet plant firmly, body leans forward, twists from the waist, swings, and shoots. So that those who practise more genteel sports might understand, this means we are sending a government representative to the United States to gather all data and evidence relating to Hungary. We will then urgently establish the constitutional and legal framework to ensure we do not have to stand idly by whilst pseudo-civil society organisations serve foreign interests right before our eyes and organise political actions under the guise of ‘ ’. We must not stand idly by whilst they pocket their pay before our very eyes, flaunting their impunity by invoking and relying on their international protection. “Miklós put up with it, put up with it as long as he could.” We have had enough now. The American ambassador has left; international protection is over. The bell has tolled. Let there be a new law modelled on the American Magnitsky Act! Let us shut off the Soros network’s money taps; let state bodies fulfil their duty to protect sovereignty; and let us enforce the law against those who have committed offences to date. Let us let in the fresh air coming from the West. “Am I free to break new ground at Dévény with the new songs of a new era?” You are! We’ve had the opening to the East; now comes the opening to the West! Since you’ve missed it so much. With the empire’s Budapest warehouse, we might as well wrap things up by Easter. There is a political tradition of this in Hungary. Easter articles, Easter constitutions, Easter spring cleaning.
My dear friends!
However, we must manage our strength wisely. For in the meantime, we must wage a continuous and increasingly multifaceted battle against the Empire’s head in Brussels. The people of the liberal networks are now retreating to Brussels. There is a well-trodden route for this from America; it was already used during Donald Trump’s first presidency. What’s more, laws similar to ours are springing up one after another in patriotic countries—look at Israel and Georgia, and perhaps more will follow—and the liberals will head for Brussels from there too.
My dear friends!
We know that the truth is on our side against Brussels. But that is not enough. Brussels has stumbled upon the truth on several occasions, but somehow it has always picked itself up and carried on. Truth alone is not enough; we must also show strength. We are fighting five major battles simultaneously against the Brussels bureaucrats. Yet we do not like war; we are peace-loving, peace-seeking, indeed devout people. But there are matters on which we cannot yield, and we will not yield. On the issue of migration, if necessary, we will go right to the wall, and even beyond. We will never swallow the migration pact, with which Brussels would direct migrants here. We are rebelling, and we are inciting others to rebel too. The Poles and the Dutch have already stood up, the Italians are almost there, and the Germans seem to be too. And of course we cannot give in; we cannot give up on protecting our children either. It is no use dragging us before the courts in Luxembourg. In fact, I suggest we go on the counter-offensive here. Let us write into the constitution that a person is either a man or a woman. And that’s that. In fact, I advise the Pride organisers not to bother with preparations for this year’s parade. It’s a waste of money and time. It doesn’t matter what District Commander Weber and his Hungarian agents say.
Ladies and Gentlemen!
Brussels also claims that the Hungarian pension system is unsustainable, and is therefore demanding the withdrawal of the thirteenth-month pension. But the truth is that the Hungarian pension system is sustainable if everyone continues to work and if we keep wages on an upward trajectory. And that is precisely what we want; our goal is an average income of one million forints. Of course, they know this in Brussels too. In reality, they want us not to spend our money on the thirteenth-month pension, but to hand it over to multinational companies. We’re sorry, Herr Weber, the thirteenth-month pension is staying.
And at the same time, they are demanding the abolition of utility bill reductions. They can say this with inimitable elegance: ‘Let us abolish regulations that hinder market pricing.’ My friends, this is no trivial matter. For millions of families, the utility bill reductions mean survival. Let me give you the figures. What amounts to a 250,000 forint bill a year here is 600,000 in Romania, 650,000 in Slovakia, 900,000 in Poland, and over a million forints in the Czech Republic. Not to mention Austria, where utility bills are sky-high. This is what would await us too if we let Brussels have its way. But Minister Lantos will not let that happen.
And finally, there is the matter of Ukraine. This is not about the war itself, but about what comes after it. The war is following its own course towards its conclusion. In reality, the war is not even about Ukraine, but about bringing the territory known as Ukraine – which until now has been a buffer zone, a buffer state between NATO and Russia – under NATO supervision from now on. Why European and American liberals thought the Russians would stand idly by and watch this remains a mystery to this day. It is now clear that the attempt has failed. Ukraine, or what remains of it, will once again become a buffer zone. It will not be a member of NATO. But will it be a member of the European Union? That is for the Hungarians to decide. Against the will of Hungary and the Hungarian people, Ukraine will never become a member of the European Union. Ukrainian accession would ruin Hungarian farmers, and not just them, but the entire Hungarian national economy.
My dear friends!
We must also achieve a breakthrough in the Hungarian economy. We must preserve jobs, which will not be easy. Here, hanging over our heads, is the looming storm cloud of a tariff war. We cannot prevent it; this is the big boys’ league. But we must work out where the lightning will strike – that is, where there will be redundancies, factory closures and other economic woes. There are countries here in Europe that have no chance of avoiding trouble, let alone planning a breakthrough. We have a good chance. We must fight for our factories, both those already producing here and those currently seeking their place in the world. I remind you of those debates when the left disparagingly labelled Hungarian car factories as mere assembly plants and attacked our industrial policy. Today, the new US President wants to acquire these factories and relocate them to America. I do not believe he is collecting rubbish. Services, the knowledge-based economy and tourism are important, but no country can survive without manufacturing, that is, a labour-based economy. Our aim is that, whilst factories are closing down around the world and tens of thousands of people are being laid off, even in Germany, in Hungary we will develop, expand and even hire new workers. The best defence is attack. That is why we are announcing the ‘100 New Factories’ programme, because this is the only way we can ensure that every Hungarian who wants to work will have a job in the future. Ministers Szijjártó and István Nagy are in for a busy year.
My friends!
We must also ensure that, amidst all this struggle, we do not lose sight of the future or confine ourselves to the present. By 2030 – and that is only a few years away – technological changes will usher in a new world where, for the first time in human history, there will be more computers than human brains in manufacturing, more artificial sensors than human eyes, and more robotic arms than human labour. This is as significant as electricity was a hundred years ago. We are not doing badly in terms of preparation, but we need to step up the pace. This is illustrated by the fact that a few years ago, Volkswagen was the world’s largest car manufacturer. Today, with 670,000 workers, they produce 8 million cars, whilst Toyota produces 11 million with 380,000 workers. A third fewer workers, a third more cars! In Germany, tens of thousands are set to be made redundant, whilst 7 departments are working on the future of nuclear technology and 130 on gender theory studies. We will not make this mistake; we will have enough jobs, and we will also have enough workers trained for the new industry. However, preparations must be accelerated, and the government knows what it needs to do. It is time to put László Palkovics back to work.
Ladies and Gentlemen!
We are also announcing Europe’s largest tax cut programme. If we’re going to make a breakthrough, let’s make it count. We will double the tax allowance for children in two stages. First stage: 1 July. Second stage: 1 January 2026. Parents will be able to deduct 20,000 forints for one child, 80,000 forints for two children, and 200,000 forints for three or more children from their tax and social security contributions. This affects more than a million families. We are introducing full income tax exemption for CSED and GYED benefits. We are introducing full, lifelong income tax exemption for mothers of two and three children. For those with three children, this will be introduced in one go, from October 2025. For those with two children, this will be phased in from January 2026. This is a global sensation; there is nothing like it anywhere else. It is a huge expenditure, but the growing economy, business support programmes and full employment together will be able to generate this amount whilst simultaneously reducing the budget deficit and public debt. It has long been our dream that parents raising children should not be at a financial disadvantage compared to those who do not have children. Those who have children know that what they lose in their pockets by raising a child, they gain in their hearts. If you raise a decent person, you will ultimately benefit financially as well. But that will take time; it will only happen after many years. That is why it is right to grant tax exemptions even to those raising minor children. I am also convinced that more children will be born if mothers can feel financially secure with their children. Had we not introduced the new family support system in 2010, there would be 200,000 fewer children in Hungary today. Imagine what it would be like, where we would be, if these 200,000 Hungarian children had not been born.
Ladies and gentlemen!
COVID, the war, energy prices and food inflation have taken their toll on families; it is time for them to find a safe haven. That is why we need a breakthrough in home-building too. What is already in place: Csok Plus, Falusi Csok, the reduced VAT rate on home purchases and the rural home renovation programme, and housing support is now available from employers. From 1 April, this will be joined by a 5 per cent cap on mortgage interest rates. The Szép Card is on its way, as are voluntary pension fund savings. The Student City is also coming, which I expect will provide 18,000 places in halls of residence.
Ladies and Gentlemen!
President Reagan told his ministers, ‘I hate two things: communists and taxes; do something about them.’ We can safely add inflation to that list. All the breakthroughs in politics and the economy will be in vain if we cannot curb inflation. Inflation can undermine the success of our other programmes and make people’s lives miserable. Food inflation, in particular. Alongside the hundred new factories, tax cuts and home-building programmes, we therefore need a fourth: a programme to curb inflation. You may recall that we have already introduced price-capping measures once before. A food price freeze, a price monitoring system, mandatory special offers, and in the meantime we raised and raised and raised wages. It is a logical idea that the best defence against price rises is higher pay. This is generally true. But it is not always enough, nor is it sufficient in all circumstances. Here and now, for example, it is not enough. What has happened is that retailers and supermarket chains have significantly raised the prices of certain basic foodstuffs, and with that, of course, their profits too. In January this year, milk was 39 per cent more expensive, eggs 35 per cent more expensive, and cooking oil 11 per cent more expensive. That is a lot! In fact, it is unacceptable! That is why we will not allow it. I have instructed Minister Márton Nagy to reach an agreement with the retail chains to halt the rise in prices – through friendly persuasion. But if friendly persuasion does not work, we will resort to official price controls. Nobody likes price controls, but in such circumstances there is no other choice. If no agreement is reached, official prices will be imposed. And if that is not enough, we will also limit the level of retail profit . I would not like to have to go that far, because peace is better, and an agreement is better. Pensioners deserve special attention, because food prices take a larger proportion of their pensions than wages do of workers’ pay. Therefore, for pensioners, we will refund VAT on vegetables, fruit and dairy products up to a certain monthly amount in the second half of the year. A VAT reduction would mainly increase the profits of retail chains, which is why we are not opting for that; a VAT refund, on the other hand, will certainly reach those for whom it is intended, which is why we will be introducing this.
My dear friends!
I see we have a few minutes left. Let’s talk about politics too. This is where the Chief of the General Staff comes in. I recommend moderation to everyone. I would have advised him the same. Party politics must be kept out of the army, not brought in. In the army, there is only room for national strategy. Officers must know that this stands far above party politics. Rivalries, villa scandals and ego clashes are unworthy of the armed forces; they have no place in the public eye, and certainly not on the political stage. I expect Minister Szalay-Bobrovniczky to ensure that everyone in the army acts accordingly. Honour to the Hungarian soldiers!
Then there are the worrying figures on the rise in drug trafficking and consumption. There is a problem here. Cheap, toxic concoctions and synthetic drugs have flooded the country. We must put a stop to this. At any cost. Literally at any cost. I will appoint a special government commissioner. We are introducing a zero-tolerance policy. And I ask Minister Pintér to order a manhunt against drug dealers. Drug dealers ruin and kill other people’s children, so they deserve neither mercy nor leniency. Nor will they receive any.
My dear friends!
We support the parliamentary motion to enshrine the right to cash in the constitution. Cash is a matter of freedom. Therefore, its use is not a custom, but a right. I hear that digital money is the future. That may well be, but only cash can be a real, tangible safeguard. We do not want to be slaves to the banks. The bank card belongs to the bank; cash belongs to you. We await Minister Lázár’s parliamentary motion.
Dear Friends!
I sense a serious debate brewing over the right of small settlements to self-defence. Do small settlements have the right to protect their rural character and identity? If so, let us give them the tools to enforce that right and put a stop to urban sprawl. The countryside, the village, the small town is not a testing ground, but a heritage. Honourable Minister Navracsics, let us therefore grant them the right to self-defence!
And finally, ladies and gentlemen, let us spare two minutes for the opposition as well. Good humour is key. It seems our opponents are making threats again. We do not make threats, but we do not like being threatened either. We wouldn’t recommend it to anyone, lest we end up taking it seriously. For the sake of order, we propose that Members of the European Parliament, including the current ones, should also be required to submit the kind of declaration of assets that the law requires of us, Members of the Hungarian Parliament. We encourage Mr Máté Kocsis, the group leader, to do so.
My friends!
Let us never forget that our true adversary is not the Hungarian opposition, but their masters. The Hungarian opposition is merely carrying out orders, merely serving the imperial will that funds, feeds and directs them. How many times have we seen them in our history, in various guises, as bought-off, subservient lackey politicians fawning at imperial courts. They were always whatever suited their interests at the time: Zapadniks, Muscovites, Labancs. They were whatever was worth it for them at the time; they were never Hungarians, never patriots. Well, now we’ve got them again, only this time in Brussels-style garb. All that matters to Brussels is that it has a government in Hungary that submits to it. A government that does not build a fence, does not tax multinationals and banks, does not pass child protection laws, does not introduce a thirteenth-month pension or utility bill cuts, but instead allows the country to be plundered nicely, swiftly and thoroughly, just as they are wont to do. They’re always looking for the right person for this. They’ve already tried an expert prime minister, a Canadian-Hungarian dual national, a mayor with hyper-passive language skills, and a coalition cobbled together from the far left to the far right. Now there’s a new show, a new stage, a new puppet – only the hand is the same, and the familiar smile. Until 1990, Moscow guaranteed impunity to the communists; now Brussels guarantees impunity to the liberals. How fortunate that Brussels is not Moscow! For us, a daily fine of one million for keeping migrants out; for our opponents, impunity for common criminal offences. Immunity in exchange for an oath of allegiance. But how will this end? Let us listen to János Arany. “The night beetle buzzes, crashes into the wall. It makes a loud thud, then falls silent.”
God above us all, Hungary above all else!
Come on, Hungary, come on, Hungarians!
