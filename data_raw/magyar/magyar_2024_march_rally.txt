176 years ago, on this very day, just a few hundred metres from here, at the Pilvax Café, a group of young Hungarians – Sándor Petőfi, Pál Vasvári, Mihály Tompa, József Irinyi, Frigyes Korányi and their companions – dreamed of a new world, a new homeland. And where the four most important words were peace, freedom, equality and brotherhood. My friends, the revolutionaries of 1848, set an example of unity. They represented the entire Hungarian nation. Among them were Catholics, Calvinists, Lutherans, Orthodox Christians, Jews and atheists. There were royalists and republicans, liberals and conservatives, and alongside the Hungarian majority, the Swabians, Serbs, Saxons, Slovaks and, to some extent, the Romanians also fought against the oppressive powers. Their common goal was the creation of a free, modern, sovereign, European Hungary. They taught us never to lose hope, never to accept that something cannot be changed. Let us show that we – conservatives, civic democrats, social democrats and liberals – are, first and foremost, Hungarians. 
Hello, my friends! Let us say it loud and clear: things cannot go on as they have for the past 30 years. Hungary is in the midst of a moral, political and economic crisis. The majority of the Hungarian people have lost confidence in the entire political elite that has ruled the country for the past 30 years. Hungarians no longer trust government officials who treat the country as their own fiefdom, nor the equally discredited opposition politicians. They have no desire for unnecessary ideological and social division. More and more people are realising that the rifts and disputes artificially created by politicians are merely intended to conceal the workings of the power machine and the siphoning off of national wealth to a narrow circle of cronies and family members. We have the Hungarian political elite and the oligarchs associated with them to thank for this. Since our accession to the EU twenty years ago, tens of thousands of billions of forints in EU funds have flowed into our country. Half of this money was used to rebuild Germany, which had been reduced to rubble in the Second World War, and to establish Europe’s leading economic power. What has happened here? 
In the space of a decade, despite EU funding, our country has become the second poorest and most corrupt in the EU. This has created a circle that can only be entered with a fortune of hundreds of billions, where politics and the economy are intertwined, and where our national treasures—hotels, companies, farmland, the shores of Lake Balaton, motorways, the airport, and the list goes on until morning—are flowing out of control. The first and most important step for the new government must be for Hungary to join the European Public Prosecutor’s Office. We will strengthen the oversight of the use of EU funds, and as a second step, if he has not stepped down by then, we will replace the current chief prosecutor so that the office can carry out its work free from political influence. One of the Orbán government’s key messages is support for families. Yet last year saw the lowest number of births in our country since the change of regime. One reason for this is the absence of the hundreds of thousands of our compatriots who have left the country and the tens of thousands of Hungarian children born abroad. In addition to happy relationships, what is needed above all is a peaceful, liveable country where stability and predictability prevail, with an inspiring vision for the future. 
Education and healthcare are two other key issues where immediate and radical change will be needed. Everyone agrees that without quality education and healthcare, there is no future for Hungary. The respect for the teaching profession has been devalued to an unprecedented extent, the right to strike has been rendered impossible, and meanwhile the performance of Hungarian students has been deteriorating year on year in international surveys. We demand a comprehensive reform of the education system, the involvement of professional organisations in the preparation of new regulations, and that all stakeholders be consulted. If anyone responsible for education, whether as a state secretary or a minister, is unable to recognise that it is no longer the Prussian-style educational model based on rote learning and homework that goes on until late in the evening that serves the future of our children and, by extension, our country , then they are certainly unfit for the task. Thank you for ensuring that one of the new government’s key priorities will be to restore freedom in education and respect for teachers. Hungarian schools will educate young people to be creative, free-thinking and capable of critical thinking; in other words, we will prepare them for real life. 
Perhaps no one living in Hungary needs to be told about the deplorable conditions prevailing in the health and social sectors. Underpaid nurses, overworked doctors, long waiting lists, the exodus of young, well-trained doctors abroad, unfilled GP and specialist practices, and conditions in many places resembling medieval infirmaries. A complete lack of preventive screenings and campaigns promoting a healthy lifestyle. Here too, we are in the eleventh hour. A radical overhaul of the healthcare system and the removal of the so-called ‘doctor barons’ cannot wait a single minute, because any delay could cost human lives. If you ask where the new government will find the financial resources to reform the healthcare system, I would say that if the 1,309 billion forints spent on Rogán-style propaganda over the past few years were to remain in the coffers in future, that could provide a solid foundation for saving the healthcare system. In recent months, it has become clear to the whole country that the child protection system in our country is also dysfunctional and severely underfunded. Let us say it again: the Hungarian government has failed to guarantee the right of the most vulnerable children to a safe life. The Hungarian government still refuses to discuss the funding of the child protection sector. 
But my dear friends; in the past month and a half, they have not even managed to apologise to the victims of Bicske. that Russia has attacked Ukraine, causing the deaths of countless people and forcing millions to flee their homes. As regards Hungary’s security, it is of paramount importance that our country is once again regarded as a reliable NATO ally. It is also worth saying a few words about the relationship between the European Union and Hungary. Personally, I consider the opposition’s view – that for many years they have been striving to have Brussels freeze the EU funds due to Hungary – to be mistaken. The fundamental aim of the EU’s creation was never to influence the domestic politics of member states, but to facilitate and improve the lives of EU citizens in every respect. What we can do is ensure effective oversight of the use of EU funds and hold those responsible for irregularities to account. I served as the Hungarian government’s diplomat in Brussels for eight years, and I saw first-hand that the narrative is true: there is a double standard within the Brussels bubble towards the member states that joined twenty years ago. I believe that every Hungarian Member of the European Parliament must work within the EU institutions in the interests of the Hungarian people and businesses, and must yet constructively with Brussels. Hungary and Europe cannot be separated. We do not dispute the importance of maintaining good diplomatic relations with the world’s major powers. But at the end of the day, we were, are, and wish to remain Hungarians and Europeans. And whatever faults the EU may have, we are members of this club. As Lajos Kossuth said, everything for the people, everything with the people, nothing about the people, nothing above the people. That is democracy. If Hungarian voters finally see a genuine, incorruptible, unblackmailable, honest, straightforward, open political force, free from extremism, then they will increasingly come to believe that, after decades of a two-party political rotation system and a political quagmire, there is hope for change. Perhaps sooner than many would think. It must be recognised that maintaining the current political situation is solely in the interests of the power-brokers, as well as the compromised figures in both the government and the opposition. So that, hiding behind propaganda and sham debates, they can continue to hand over the nation’s wealth to cronies and family members . To address the aforementioned problems and the challenges facing our country, my proposal is as follows. 
We will establish a centrist political force that will put an end to the logic of the artificially maintained partisan camps of recent decades. We will establish a force that any well-meaning Hungarian who wishes to work for their country can join. One day we will launch the ‘Standing Hungarians’ community, and shortly afterwards I will announce the associated political party, with which, and with your help, we will reclaim our homeland, our symbols and our expressions, and together we will build a modern, European Hungary where everyone is equal and where every Hungarian person’s voice truly counts. Everything depends on faith, good intentions and determination. As US President John Fitzgerald Kennedy said, ask not what your country can do for you, but what you can do for your country. There are already so many of us, and I believe that as soon as the news of this new hope spreads from Záhony to Őri Szentpéter, from Makó to Hegyeshalom, more and more people will join us, and soon perhaps the entire Hungarian nation will declare together that we should build a new homeland, where what matters is not whether someone is conservative or liberal, religious or atheist, married or single, poor or rich, but 
that we are all Hungarians and we love our country. Hello, my friends! I do not promise eternal life or free beer, but neither do I expect blood, sweat or tears. I promise that together, in good spirits, listening to one another and trusting one another, we will build a new homeland brick by brick – a modern European country where it is good to be born, good to live and good to raise children. Thank you for the tens of thousands of messages of support, suggestions and expressions of intent to join us. We face a huge task in the coming months to create a political force that is both effective and resilient, capable of winning the trust of the Hungarian people and restoring genuine hope to our compatriots. If you feel the strength and determination within yourselves to change our country and the future of our children and grandchildren, then we warmly welcome you into this community. Many accuse me of having been sent here. No. I came because I felt that enough was enough. Just as nobody sent you either, but you came because you felt that change was needed. 
Building a community and a political party, and transforming a country, is a huge undertaking. It can only be achieved by working together as a team. I promise that we will do everything in our power to ensure that exemplary people take the lead in our community, and I promise that, however far we go with God’s help, we will remain with you and will always be open to your questions, your suggestions and, of course, your criticism. I believe that together we are capable of shaking ourselves up, standing up once more, looking each other in the eye, and, holding hands, saying that together we will succeed. Thank you for coming. I ask you to believe in one another and in your homeland. Do not be afraid, and lend a hand. If you do so, the Hungarian name will once again be beautiful, worthy of its former great renown. Whatever the centuries have smeared upon us, we shall wash away the disgrace. We swear by the God of the Hungarians, we swear that we shall no longer be slaves. Thank you very much.
