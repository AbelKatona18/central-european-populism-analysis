It was raining almost everywhere in the country. Men and women rose early, at the break of day. It was a morning just like any other. In Pest, in a tenement block, a young man looked into the chipped mirror whilst shaving. In Timișoara, a middle-class woman with many children saw her face reflected in a bowl whilst drinking her morning coffee. In the Highlands, in a castle, a farmer in his late thirties gazed at a carefully polished, gleaming silver spoon. On the Great Plain, a young beauty who had recently married was drawing water from the well, and in the reflection of the water she caught sight of herself. In Komárom, a veteran soldier was adjusting his moustache in a sword polished to a shine. It was morning. Everyone was going about their business. Everyone was doing what they usually did. No one, not a single soul, thought that something would change irrevocably that very day. But suddenly, out of nowhere, they heard an inner voice. The voice rang out crystal clear. My sweet homeland, how did you get into such trouble? Where is the freedom we so desired? Where is the life befitting a Hungarian, 
that so many of us long for? They froze, staring at their own reflections for a long time. Minutes passed in stillness. A state of alert, of responsibility. The final moment before the change. By the time they looked up, they knew they could no longer remain silent. They knew they could no longer remain silent. They knew that the homeland needed them. Rise up, Hungarians! The homeland calls! The time is now, it is now or never! The five of them knew this, but not only they, but many millions of Hungarians across the country at the same time. That morning, before the miracle, it spread across the Carpathian Basin, from the highest mountains to the Great Plain, from the Alps to the Carpathians, from the source of the Tisza to its mouth. The miracle was that a nation’s heart beat as one, awoke together, and thought as one. The Hungarians pinned cockades to their hearts and recited together: ‘We were slaves until now, our forefathers were damned. Those who lived and died in freedom cannot rest in a land of servitude. My friends, 1848 belonged to every Hungarian, for freedom is the ancestral right of every Hungarian. Men and women. 
Young and old, poor and wealthy, mothers, poets, soldiers, day labourers and students. Those who spoke Hungarian, German, Romani or Armenian, but all of them Hungarians yearning for freedom. No matter where they came from, no matter where they lived, they might have been Tóts, Kossuths, Croats, Batthyánys, Szabós, Dessefys, Kovács, Széchenyis, Germans, Táncsics, Deáks, or even Petőfis. They were all Hungarians, and they were all heroes. Hungarians who wanted to do something for their homeland. They may have been neighbours, or they may have lived hundreds of kilometres apart. An invisible thread bound them together. That thread is love for the homeland. It is love for our homeland that, even after so many years, still binds us all together today, every Hungarian person. My fellow countrymen, our ancestors’ decision was clear and resolute, yet we have had to fight, and must still fight today, time and again for freedom and national independence. And yet our choice was made over a thousand years ago. And yet, over the past thousand years, many have come from all quarters to say that what we have chosen is wrong, that what we believe in is wrong, that we should renounce our principles, betray our heroes, refrain from fighting for what is right, give up our freedom, and surrender to the superior force. 
You worthless wretch, who now, when it matters, dares not die, for whom a wretched life is dearer than the honour of the homeland. Let us repeat: for whom is a wretched life dearer than the honour of the homeland? My fellow countrymen, millions of our ancestors gave their lives and their blood. They fought and fell for a free Hungarian Europe. That is why we say it again, so that the fearful emperor stuck in the past and the worthless Janissaries lurking in his entourage may hear it too. Our homeland is part of the West, our homeland is part of the European community, our homeland is part of NATO. Not because of treaties or legal clauses, but because it is written in our destiny; our ancestors bequeathed to us where we belong. We are Hungarians in Europe. We are Hungarians who wish to live in peace. As Hungarians, we reject war and violence. We consign fear-mongering to the past, and war-mongering. We are not afraid! We have learnt from our ancestors that nothing lasts forever. We did not become Turks in 150 years. We Hungarians are not afraid! 
Hungarians, shall others control our destiny, or shall we control it ourselves? Shall power rule over us, or shall we ourselves be the power? Shall we be subjects, or citizens? The Hungarians of 1848 fought for a single goal: they wanted to be free. They wanted nothing else, only to be free. They simply wanted to be free. Free – just a few letters; the existence of freedom hardly seems to register, yet its absence kills. The Hungarians wanted to be free; they wanted to live freely. They wanted to be able to decide their own fate, not the Emperor, not the landlord, not the county governor, but the freely elected Hungarian Parliament. They wanted there to be no privileges granted at birth, no distinction between one Hungarian and another, equality before the law at last, a shared burden, genuine freedom of the press, and no censorship. That is all. That is all the Hungarians wanted. Subject or Hungarian citizen? That was the question then, and it is the same question today. My fellow countrymen! The man of the past led the freedom-loving Hungarians by the nose for a long time. He lied to our faces. 
They deceived us. They despicably exploited a nation’s desire to finally become free Hungarian citizens. Yet they never, ever meant it seriously. Every word, every claim was merely a political ploy. They laughed behind the backs of those who believed their words. They toasted the misled Hungarians with the most expensive French champagne. They saw only another weapon in every word they uttered: the weapon of division. To divide, so that they might continue to steal. Because they never, ever wanted self-aware Hungarian citizens. They never wanted to unite the nation or heal the wounds of history. Never. They wanted subjects who would not talk back. Subjects they could keep in fear. Subjects they could poison with a foreign past. Subjects they could provoke. Subjects, just like their Turkish, Austrian, or even Communist predecessors. My fellow countrymen, Hungarians at home and across the world. The past has forced world wars, tyrants, destroyers and malefactors upon our homeland. But we, Hungarian citizens building the future, are a stubborn people. 
Time and again we rise up, for we know this is our only path. For we know that a sword shines brighter than a chain, adorns the arm better, and yet we have borne chains; here with you, our old sword. We uphold the message of ’48, and choose freedom over bondage. We choose to be citizens, not subjects, in our own country. We demand that every Hungarian person be a true citizen in this country. Not just the privileged, not just the wealthy, but every Hungarian person. Free Hungarian citizens. All of us. My friends, a citizen is someone who is entitled to the best possible state healthcare, wherever they live; someone who is entitled not to die from diseases that could have been prevented in time; someone who is entitled not to have to wait years for a simple examination; someone who is entitled to the best education, wherever they live in the country. A citizen is someone who is free to choose in which field to develop their talents. A citizen is someone whom the state, to which they pay their taxes, protects from tyrannical politicians, polluting factories, paedophile monsters and all criminals. 
A citizen is someone who is entitled to a decent wage; a citizen is someone who is entitled to a decent pension; a citizen is someone who is free to think as they please, free to start a business, free to choose their faith, and who is not persecuted or discriminated against because of their worldview. A citizen is someone who respects the law and knows that its letter applies to everyone in the same way as it does to the law enforcement officers. A citizen is someone who is respected by their elected leaders, over whom no one rules and from whom no one steals their money. No one in this country dares to call someone a traitor if they think differently from the majority. A citizen is someone who does not allow their own leaders to call them an insect to be crushed. And let us build a future together in which every Hungarian person can finally be a citizen in their own country. Every Hungarian person. The Tisza government, to be formed in the spring of 2026, will therefore work round the clock to ensure that every Hungarian citizen can be a citizen in their own country. We will bring home the 8,000 billion forints in EU funding to which Hungarians are entitled. 
We will introduce a wealth tax system to help bridge the gap. And yes, we will not shy away from imposing a wealth tax on billionaires either. We will provide 200,000 forints on a health card for those on lower pensions and for the generations rebuilding our country, which can be used for food, medicines and healthcare, amongst other things. We will provide a 100,000-forint back-to-school grant for every child. Instead of the Orbán Plan, which is based on hospital closures that have come to light, we will develop the healthcare system. We will spend at least 500 billion forints more on this sector each year, and we will also support smaller rural hospitals. By bringing EU funds home, restoring trust, curbing rampant corruption and making rational use of state revenues, we will finally restart the Hungarian economy. Thank you! On the ruins of the Defence Office, we will establish the National Asset Recovery and Defence Office. We will put an end to the propaganda that poisons our souls. Those who have stirred up trouble and schemed must be held to account, for only then can we finally turn over a new leaf. 
We must not allow one man’s lust for power to derail our country. We will immediately cap the Prime Minister’s term of office at two terms and put this rule up for debate. Our 140-page programme sets out the first crucial steps towards a functioning and humane Hungary. It is a programme designed to ensure that every Hungarian can finally be an equal and free citizen in their own country, and that Hungarians can once again be brothers and sisters to one another. We Hungarians who care for our country know that we can only succeed together. We know there is no right or left. We know there is no such thing as a ‘good’ or ‘better’ Hungarian, that it does not matter whether someone is from the countryside, a city, young or old. Everyone, everyone can do something for their country. We know full well that we belong together. We know that whatever the terrorists try, the ailing emperor of Hatvanpuszta will no longer succeed. They will no longer be able to pit us against one another. We are already together; we have crossed the divides of division and joined hands. 
The cure is simple: love for our homeland. This is what surrounds us, binds us together and protects us. The young teacher from Budapest, shaving, who has no money left for the last days of the month after paying his rent and utility bills. The nurse raising her four children alone, sipping her morning coffee in Nagykanizsa, who worries both for her patients and about how her older children are getting on in London, and when she might see them again. A young woman from Jázboldogháza, newly married, waiting for the train that is always late to take her home, glancing at the government’s hate posters as she waits. She, who does not want to work in toxic battery factories, and who knows that her wages would never be enough to buy her own flat. A conservative, well-off entrepreneur in Vág, staring at his laptop’s dimmed screen, who doesn’t want the Fidesz henchmen to take away his business, and who would like 
performance to finally count, not connections. Or an experienced prosecutor in Budapest, glancing at his phone and eager to finally pursue criminals in accordance with his oath, rather than following political directives. They all know by now that change is needed. They all know by now that our beloved homeland is in dire straits today. They all know that we can only succeed together. They all know that the future is knocking at the door, and that just as in 1848, we can now take our destiny into our own hands . They all know that 12 April 2026 will mark the beginning of the Hungarian nation’s resurgence. In 1848–49 and 1956, our revolutions and our freedom were crushed and drowned in blood. The Russians came and stayed. They crushed the revolution, but they could never break us. They never could. Neither the Bak era nor the Kádár era could break us. 
And the Orbán government won’t either. Not even if, like his predecessors, the wannabe emperor, in his final desperation, calls in the KGB tsar against the Hungarian citizens yearning for freedom. Just like in 1849 and 1956. Orbán has invited the most highly trained Russian agents into our country to interfere in the elections and once again take away our most sacred possession, Hungarian freedom, for which our ancestors gave their lives. Viktor Orbán called in agents to ensure the will of the Hungarian people could not prevail. Lies, deception and provocation – that is what they have in store for us. My friends, Viktor Orbán has betrayed Hungarian freedom, and he has betrayed it for 30 pieces of silver – 30 pieces of silver for himself and his dynasty. Shame, shame, shame! I have some bad news for them: we will not let this happen; the Hungarian people will not tolerate it, because they have never tolerated having their freedom taken away. That is why on 12 April we will achieve a victory so resounding that it will be seen not only from the Moon, but from the Kremlin as well. 
Let the agents and provocateurs sent here, but above all their bosses, hear and know that it is over, comrades, Товарищи, конец, Russians go home! Besides betraying Hungary, he committed another crime. His third betrayal is that, instead of national unity, he chose to divide the nation. Yet he had every opportunity to do the opposite. In 2010, he could finally have put an end to the years of division. But no! Instead, he dug the trench deeper, hand in hand with His Majesty’s opposition. Viktor Orbán is a traitor who has betrayed our shared future. He has built not a country, but a fiefdom. He has not lifted up the nation, but turned it into the poorest and most corrupt country in the EU. Retaining power at any cost is all that matters to him. There are no rules left. There are no boundaries. Now, all that remains for the terrified emperor is war. To provoke with war, to threaten with war, to stir up war. This is his last weapon against the Hungarians. His final diabolical trick to stay in power. But I have some good news. The Orbán spirit is a thing of the past. The future belongs to you 
free Hungarian citizens. We Hungarians say: Orbán! We had to travel a long road before we found each other again. It was a long journey to reach the point where today we can once again hold each other’s hands with confidence, where today Hungarians do not turn their backs on one another. Many have done much to prevent this from happening. Division, digging trenches and stirring up trouble. Us and them. That is the division of the past. But we have known for a long time that there is no such thing as us and them. There is only us. Us together, all of us Hungarians. It has been a tremendous journey. At first there were only a few of us, then day by day, history’s verdict: there is neither right nor left, only Hungarian. The Alliance of Hungarians is now stronger than ever, but we will need it, because the trouble our country is in is at least as great as in the days of our forebears. To begin the healing process, we must work through the next 28 days 
like never before. For the healing to begin, we must win the referendum and the election. For our shared homeland, the shared homeland of all Hungarians. In 28 days’ time, there will be a referendum on our country. A referendum on how we wish to live. A referendum on our country’s place in the world. A referendum about ourselves. According to me. Fear remains among those who choose the Tisza, who choose a functioning, humane, European, proud, peaceful and cheerful Hungary. The advice of those who can finally live as citizens, the newlywed factory worker and the conservative small business owner. If they do not vote, we will not harm them, nor will we call them traitors, because the world of hatred, division and fear will come to an end forever with the Tisza’s victory at 7 pm on 12 April. Its era will last but a few more weeks and then come to an end. No citizen who disagrees with those in power will ever again be called a traitor. My fellow countrymen, I, Péter Magyar, swear that I will serve every Hungarian citizen, both within and beyond our borders. I swear that once and for all 
We shall put an end to the divisions that threaten the very existence of the Hungarian nation. I swear that our country’s new government will do everything in its power to heal the wounds of history and restore our nation to health. The Hungarian name will be beautiful once more, worthy of its former glory. Whatever the centuries have smeared upon it, we shall wash away the disgrace. May God help me!

Two Xs that will change everything. It would hardly have happened... if you hadn’t stood in the front line. Your bodies, Mary, for freedom. Now we just have to do it for our grandchildren, for Hungary. Go, and be as many as possible. Take not just one, but five people with you. Better safe than sorry. Because we must win; we have no other choice. 
On 15 March, I braced myself for the first major danger of my life. I couldn’t know what the future held; I didn’t even know what the next hour would bring. That I have a homeland, and I cannot and will not renounce my love for this homeland. I cannot and will not give up defending this country. How many of us felt the same at that moment. As the months passed, suspicion turned to knowledge, and knowledge to certainty. It was not me you lifted up, but the country. It was not me you lifted up, but when you came, the country; when you asked questions, gave answers, built, volunteered, worked, you showed something that, over the past 24 months, has made every feeling Hungarian heart beat faster. No oppression, intimidation or arrogance on the part of those in power can stop the Hungarian people now, because you are the driving force. If anyone tells you today that the Hungarian people are tired, tell them that this country has awakened and is full of a desire to act. If anyone tells you that the Hungarian people are afraid, tell them that we are no longer afraid of anything or anyone. If anyone tells you today 
that the Hungarian people believe everything because of the hate-mongering propaganda they’ve had forced down their throats, ask them if they’ve ever seen a freedom-loving Hungarian. My dear friends, I have certainly seen many freedom-loving and patriotic Hungarians. That is why today I am not asking you to believe in change. I am not asking you to believe in change. We already believe in it. Indeed, we know it is coming. After all, we are standing right at its threshold. What I am asking of you is to believe in one another, in Hungarians. Look around you now! This country is so much, so much more than what they have tried to make it. The Hungarian people are so much, so much more than what they want us to see. And believe that if we stand together, we can not only bring an era to an end, but build a new one – a calmer, fairer, more humane Hungary. A homeland where, at last, we need not fear, hate or merely survive, but can truly thrive. 
Hungary is the home of glorious revolutions, the land of everyday Hungarian heroes, and now it is asking for our help once more, and you and I, together, will help it. My dear friends, look at yourselves, look at one another; what a feeling it is to be writing history here on Heroes’ Square. In the shadow of our ancestors, you are the heroes the country is waiting for. So... the love and the desire for action with which you embrace and lift up this country is the most wonderful thing that could happen to our homeland. It is the most beautiful story, and we are only at the beginning of it. But do not forget: you are writing Hungary’s future and history, in these days, in these hours, not only with enthusiasm, but also with responsibility. We are responsible for all those who came before us. We are responsible for the legacy of Saint Stephen, Ilona Zrínyi, the Hunyadis, Sándor Petőfi, and Ferenc Deák. And we are responsible for all those who come after us. For the fate of our children, our grandchildren, and the next generation. For all those 
who are here with us today, here in this square, in our hearts. That is precisely why I ask you to work with this sense of responsibility, so that we can look our children in the eye when they ask what we have done for our country. Fight as if there were no second chance. Let March resound! And let April flow. We have twenty-eight days. These twenty-eight days are life itself. Twenty-eight days when everything counts. Every conversation, every leaflet, every knock on a door. Every person we tell that the fate of our country now depends on us. Don’t wait for anyone, don’t wait for a miracle. We are the ones who will make the miracle happen. You will create the miracle together. Over the next 28 days, talk to people, your friends, your family, your neighbours and your colleagues. Even those who have so far just shrugged it off. Even those who have so far been afraid. And especially those who have not yet dared to believe in change. 
Because deep down, they too know that they may try to divide us, they may try to tear us apart, but there is something we share. From Alsó-Szentmárton to Mégkerék, from Mohács to Komárom, we love our country very, very much, and we would very much like to save it. Take away the message that we will no longer be subjugated. Take away the message that we are all free Hungarian citizens. We are free Hungarian citizens. My fellow countrymen, that is why working for this incomparable country is a wonderful feeling. No one, no one on the face of the earth knows or can feel what we do. That being Hungarian is such a heart-wrenching, uplifting, incomparable feeling. That heroes gave their lives for this homeland, for every single part of it, and now we want to live and act for this homeland. And we will live and act for it. With the same determination with which we have seen these two years through, and with which we will see the 28 days ahead of us through, and with which we will save, restore and make this beautiful country flourish. 
You know me, perhaps all too well. If I need to speak to people, I’ll be there. If I need to go from village to village, I’ll go. If I need to stand up for this country, I’ll stand up anywhere. But I cannot get through these twenty-eight days without you. Help me! If you come with me, we’ll walk every street in the country. If you come with me, we’ll bring the news to every Hungarian that life can be different. If you come with me, we’ll show that this country is no longer afraid. I’m ready. Will you come with me? Can we see this through? Give us back our country! Thank you! There are 28 days left, 28 times 24 hours, 28 days and 28 nights. You can be sure of one thing: I’ll be here. We won’t be sleeping much, but we’ll be working all the more. I suggest we get straight down to it. From now on, no more whingeing, no more holding back, no more rationing our strength – just work. Just work, at the end of which we will reclaim our stolen homeland, and together, step by step, we will heal Hungary and fulfil 
what our ancestors wanted: a free, independent and civil Hungary. My dear friends, spring, the Tisza, change and youth cannot be stopped. Sunlight always breaks through the darkness. We swear by the God of the Hungarians, we swear that we shall no longer be prisoners. Let there finally be freedom and harmony!
