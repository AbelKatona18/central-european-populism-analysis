My fellow countrymen and women, Hungarians! We’ve done it! And Hungary has won this election. Not just by a narrow margin, but by a landslide. In fact, by a massive landslide. Together, we have ousted the Orbán regime; together, we have liberated Hungary; we have reclaimed our homeland. Thank you, thank you all – we could not have done this without you. Our victory is visible not from the moon, but from every Hungarian window. Whether it be the smallest thatched cottage or a high-rise block of flats, in a big city or the countryside, it is visible from every Hungarian window. Thank you very much! Never before in the history of democratic Hungary have so many people voted, and never before has any party received such a strong mandate as Tisza. Thank you for the approximately 3.3 million votes with which you have today given us a historic mandate to govern, to build a functioning and humane homeland for all of us, for every Hungarian. Tisza has not only won this election, but all signs indicate that we will have a strong two-thirds majority in the new National Assembly. 
This is the mandate that will enable the transition to be as efficient, peaceful and smooth as possible. With grateful hearts, we thank all 3.3 million people who placed their trust in us. I would like to thank my family, my sons; I thank my colleagues; we thank, thank, thank our more than 50,000 volunteers for the superhuman work they have done over the past two years. We thank our candidates, our experts and every Hungarian who supported the change of regime in any way. Today, my dear friends, you have worked a miracle; today, Hungary has made history. Yet together we have shown everyone that even if the galley is above and the water’s current below, it is still the water that reigns supreme. We have done all this against a headwind, as we have said so many times, like millions of humble labourers. We have triumphed, even though the ruling party deployed its entire apparatus against us. In a manner reminiscent of the one-party state, it attempted to destroy the Tisza with the secret services; they spent hundreds of billions on a campaign of lies, on inciting hatred, 
and to turn Hungarians against Hungarians. But this too was their downfall. This too was their downfall. Tonight it has been proven that love—love for our homeland—has overcome the profound contempt and the arrogance of power that today’s would-be tyrants feel towards the Hungarian people. For, my dear compatriots, as Lajos Kossuth said, one cannot imagine a more accursed, more vile sin than to play with the blood of citizens and the peace of a nation. This is the sin over which the Hungarian people have passed judgement today. Today, truth has triumphed over lies. We have won today because the Hungarian people did not ask what their country could do for them, but asked what they could do for their country. You did not merely ask the question; you also found the answer. Indeed, you did not merely find it, but you rose to the challenge demanded by the nation. 
Today, Hungarians have said no to deception, no to lies, no to misdirection, and no to betrayal. My dear friends, two years ago a few people set out, and in the end, in the battle between David and Goliath, love triumphed, because in the end, love always triumphs. Thank you all for heeding the biblical advice not to be afraid. Thank you for believing that we can change our destiny. Thank you for believing that we, the Hungarian people, are the ones who shape our own history. Thank you, thank you, thank you. And yet how many times and how many people told us that it was not worth going to the countryside, that it was not worth it and impossible to leave the capital. How many times did we hear that we must come to terms with His Majesty’s opposition, that we must make deals, because there is no other way. Not at all! You have proved to everyone that yes, it is possible. My friends, as Lajos Kossuth also put it, the logic of history sometimes lingers for a long time, sometimes leaps centuries in a single moment, leaps so great that the short-sighted would cry out in disbelief and speak of chance. There are such moments of grace in Hungarian history. 
Such was 15 March 1848, such was 23 October 1956, and yes, let us say it boldly, such a moment of grace is today, 12 April 2026. May this day too be a date inscribed in gold in the annals of Hungarian freedom. It is not the victory of one party over another, but the victory of the Hungarian people over those who betrayed them, the victory of freedom over oppression, the victory of truth over lies. Let this current victory belong to all Hungarians, both those who voted for Tisza and those who did not. Yes, it is a brilliant and wonderful victory for every Hungarian. But every Hungarian feels in their heart that this is a wonderful, shared victory. A victory because our country has decided and wants to live again. It wants to be a European country once more. A country that is not a vassal to anyone. A country where achievement counts. A country where everyone can be a Hungarian citizen. A country where citizens can count on their government. A country where everyone is entitled to proper healthcare, quality education, a carefree childhood, 
and a dignified old age. No one is stigmatised for thinking differently. For thinking differently from the majority. No one is stigmatised for loving differently or in a different way from the majority. For believing in something different from the majority. A country where it is not who you know that matters, but what kind of person you are. A country where you can once again happily and peacefully have children and start a family. Where you can finally come home. Dear compatriots living abroad, thank you for voting in your tens of thousands at the diplomatic missions, and thank you to the tens of thousands of you who have travelled thousands of kilometres to help your homeland today. Thank you, thank you, thank you. I ask you to come home, to move home in as large numbers as possible, because every Hungarian is needed. The task is enormous, but we Hungarians love a challenge. A country where the police, the armed forces and the secret service serve exclusively the Hungarian people. 
A state that looks after its people, especially the most vulnerable. A country that helps everyone catch up. A country that looks after every Hungarian. A Hungary that loves, respects and looks after its citizens. My fellow countrymen! We have lived here for 1,100 years, in this wonderful Carpathian Basin. In this place, which is the most beautiful on the face of the earth for us. The most beautiful mountains, the most beautiful rivers, the most beautiful lakes, the Great Plain, the Bakony, the Börzsöny, the Bükk, the Somló, the Homokhátság, the Alpokalja, the Mátra and the Fertőhánság. Our homeland, our home, our everything, the graves of our ancestors, the future of our children and grandchildren, our dreams, our tales, our prayers, our mother tongue – everything that makes us human originates from here, from our beloved homeland. My fellow countrymen, it is a wonderful thing to be Hungarian today. Everyone longs for peace, for national reunification, for a functioning and humane Hungary. It is on this path that we set out today. The Tisza government will represent every Hungarian, every single Hungarian, because – and I say this slowly – 
it is the duty and obligation of every Hungarian government. We cannot, we must not make distinctions between Hungarians, divide and incite, for that is a sin. Today, the people have said a loud and very decisive ‘yes’ to the idea that anyone who demands such a sin, anyone who divides their people, must step down from power. Today, the Hungarian people have said ‘yes’ to Europe, ‘yes’ to a free Hungary, and ‘yes’ to us representing them, helping them and putting the country in order, because this is the task of a Hungarian government, of the current Hungarian government. My fellow countrymen, we are fully aware of our responsibility and of the immense, superhuman task that lies before us. I ask you to celebrate cheerfully and peacefully today, and tomorrow we shall set to work together, for it will be our shared task to heal the wounds and clear away the ruins caused by the past decades. I ask you to join us on this journey! 
We know that the mandate we have received today is unprecedented. We know that people who would never have thought they would stand with us have voted for us today. We know, we know, and I can promise you one thing: we will work every day to earn this trust placed in us, every minute, every hour, every day, and we will serve your trust. We will have a government, a government in which every Hungarian will see that we take this mandate seriously. For we have been given a mandate to put our country in order. We have been given a mandate to build a functioning and humane Hungary, to heal the wounds of our beloved country. It will be difficult. We know the trouble is great. We see the figures; we see what our predecessor did to the Hungarian economy, to healthcare, to education, to child protection, to law enforcement, or indeed to transport. A huge, huge task lies ahead of us, but at least we have stopped the damage. I call on the outgoing Prime Minister to act as a caretaker government from today, 
and do not take any decisions that would tie the hands of the next government, the Tisza government. Should any serious decision or issue affecting our nation arise during the transition period – which we hope will be brief – please do not hesitate to contact me; you have my telephone number. Today, the Hungarian people have voted in favour of a change of regime. Therefore, those who were part of the system, its pillars and puppets, must leave public life. I call upon the President of the Republic to immediately ask me, as the leader of the winning party, to form a government, and thereafter to step down from office with whatever dignity he has left. I call upon all the puppets whom the Orbán government has imposed on the people over the past 16 years to do the same. I call on the President of the Curia to step down, as well as the President of the National Judicial Office, the Prosecutor General, the President of the Constitutional Court, the President of the State Audit Office, the President of the Competition Authority and the head of the Media Authority. 
Let them go, let them go, do not wait until we send them away, because we will send them away, because this system, which has taken over our country, our institutions and our independent authorities, is coming to an end. Immediately after their departure, with a two-thirds constitutional majority, we will restore the system of checks and balances, join the European Public Prosecutor’s Office, and guarantee We will ensure the democratic functioning of our country. Never again will we allow anyone to enslave or abandon a free Hungary. We will restore the functioning and independence of the institutions that safeguard democracy. Hungary will once again be a strong ally within the European Union and NATO. The country will once again be a strong ally representing Hungarian interests, because our homeland’s place has been, is, and will remain in Europe for a thousand years. We will place the Visegrád Four cooperation on a strong footing and, where possible, expand it. My first trip will take me to Poland to strengthen the thousand-year-old Polish-Hungarian friendship. 
My second trip will be to Vienna, and my third to Brussels, to secure the EU funds to which the Hungarian people are entitled. But most importantly, sitting in Lajos Batthyány’s chair as Prime Minister, I will represent every Hungarian person in the Carpathian Basin and across the wider world. Our Hungarian brothers and sisters abroad can count on the Tisza government just as much as those in the motherland. I have just spoken on the phone with the German Chancellor, the French President, the President of the European People’s Party, the President of the European Commission, the Croatian Prime Minister and the NATO Secretary-General. Dozens of European and other leaders are awaiting my call, and I can promise that tomorrow we will begin working with them, rebuild our alliances, and resolve contentious issues with our neighbours. The time has come to put things right and correct the mistakes of our predecessors. The time has come when we will finally no longer have to make small but deadly compromises. We will correct the mistakes, fill in the artificially dug trenches of ‘ ’, and heal the historical wounds. Wounds caused by crimes. We will begin the long-overdue process of reconciliation, 
and hold the guilty to account. There is another matter too: from now on, we will no longer be a country without consequences. Those who have plundered the country must take responsibility. Those who have stirred up hatred between Hungarians must take responsibility. Those who have betrayed the country must take responsibility. The Tisza government will free up the justice system and establish the National Asset Recovery and Protection Office. The office will be staffed by the country’s finest lawyers and investigators, and I promise they will succeed. We will never again be a country without consequences. Get ready, Captain Bence Szabó, Captain Szilveszter Pálinkás. There won’t be much rest from tomorrow. Dear young people of Hungary, let me address you separately! Thank you for bringing back hope for change, hope for a humane, courageous Hungary. You have taken action and shown what it means not to be afraid, what it means to begin building a fearless Hungary. Thank you! 
I spoke to you once, dear young people, in recent weeks, about a story: the story of the horseshoe nail that was hammered in crookedly. Well, now we can all say together that we have hammered this horseshoe nail in properly, in true Hungarian style, and with absolute certainty. Thank you very much! My dear senior citizens, thank you for not believing the lies spread by the opposition, but for knowing that I can count on you, for knowing that we will not let our pensioners become any poorer, and that we will not let families be torn apart by hatred. Thank you, thank you, and we know what your mandate means. Thank you. Thank you! Thank you! Dear fellow Fidesz supporters! I know, I know that you are disappointed today. I know that it is hard to come to terms with defeat, especially a heavy and deserved defeat. I know, I know that you may even be angry with us, perhaps with me personally, because it is hard to be in opposition. It is hard to see politicians in power whom you do not like. I know it’s hard. But I, Péter Magyar, standing here today, promise you that I will be your Prime Minister too, I will be the Prime Minister of every Hungarian person, and I will work every day of the week, 24 hours a day, to heal the wounds, so that we can accept one another, 
Even if our political views differ, I will represent you too. Just over an hour ago, the outgoing Prime Minister, Viktor Orbán, called me on the phone; Viktor Orbán called me on the phone and congratulated me on our victory. I told him, Viktor Orbán, that for a Hungarian, sitting in Lajos Batthyány’s chair is the greatest honour, the greatest responsibility, and the noblest task. And I also told him that from now on we will share the responsibility of reuniting our beloved homeland, Hungary, from tomorrow. Voters of the Tisza, dear 3,300,000 voters of the Tisza! I ask you to feel the responsibility that today’s result entails. I ask you to extend a hand of friendship from tomorrow. Let us begin the long and difficult work of reconciliation as early as tomorrow. Not with the guilty, for they must answer for their actions, but with our fellow Hungarians. Let us extend a hand to every Hungarian. Hungarians! This is the country of István Bibó, Lajos Kossuth, Görgei, and Ferenc Deák; the country of the Hunyadis, Anna Kétli, Ilona Zrínyi, and Imre Nagy; the homeland of Ferenc Rákóczi, Albert Szentgyörgyi, and Sándor Márai; the country of Puskás, Magda Szabó, 
Alfred Hajós and Péter Eszterházi, our shared homeland. And from today, this shared homeland belongs once more to every Hungarian, for there are no better or worse Hungarians, only Hungarians. And from today, this country lives again. Having once again thanked everyone for their superhuman efforts over the past two years, having thanked all our fellow Hungarians for taking part in today’s fateful election, and having thanked you, , for your support, let me conclude by quoting once more, from the bottom of my heart, the words of the first freely elected Hungarian Prime Minister. I serve, and I shall serve as long as it is of benefit to the nation. I shall do so for as long as I am able. God bless Hungary! God bless every Hungarian person!
